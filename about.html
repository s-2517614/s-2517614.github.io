About the Game

The game I'm working on is a game about exploring your inner conciousness, delving into depths of the mind that are impossible to reach in the waking world. You must solve puzzles and navigate the complex field of thoughts and feelings to get yourself to wake up. Turns out, there's a lot that goes on in your mind even in rest...

About the Story

You play as a person with a debilitating sleep disorder who has decided to participate in a clinical trial for a new sleep aid. The nurses and pharmacists are nice and plesant, the room you're in is calming, and the drugs are effective. But starting out, you have some pretty strange dreams. They assure you it's all normal, and maybe you really just haven't slept enough to experience dreams like this, but the stuff that goes on is a little more than just a side effect, you think. Or maybe it's just one hell of a placebo.

About the Gameplay

Waking Hours
When you're awake, you can wander around your room and a small part of the facility to your hearts content. Look around and see what you can find. There are some rules, though- don't go into the doctor's offices, and DON'T talk with other participants. Though, there doesn't seem to ba a lot of people here to enforce those rules.
Dream State
Your dreams are kind of...weird. You're in control of yourself (which the nurse says is kind of uncommon), but you can't seem to control the rest of your dreams. Each one is different, a different time, space, theme, what have you. Some reflect on your waking world, the others you can't seem to figure out. The one thing that's sure, though, you have to make it through to it's "conclusion" to wake up.
